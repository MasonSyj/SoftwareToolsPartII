Oncilla

^

The oncilla (Leopardus tigrinis), also known as the northern tiger cat, little spotted cat, and tigrillo, is a small spotted cat ranging from Central America to central Brazil. It is listed as Vulnerable on the IUCN Red List, and the population is threatened by deforestation and conversion of habitat to agricultural land. In 2013, it was proposed to assign the oncilla populations in southern Brazil, Paraguay, and Argentina to a new species: the southern tiger cat (L. guttulus), after it was found that it does not interbreed with the oncilla population in northeastern Brazil. The following are the traditionally recognized subspecies other than guttulus:

Leopardus tigrinus tigrinus
Leopardus tigrinus oncilla
Leopardus tigrinus pardinoides
